# Insurance Cost Benchmarks by Region and Property Type

Use these when insurance cost is not listed on the listing or in public records.
Always flag that insurance is estimated and recommend the user get a real quote.

---

## SFR Insurance (Annual Landlord Policy)

| Region | Low | Mid | High |
|---|---|---|---|
| Midwest (OH, IN, MI, MO, KS) | $900 | $1,300 | $1,800 |
| Northeast (NY, NJ, CT, MA, PA) | $1,200 | $1,800 | $2,600 |
| Southeast (GA, NC, SC, TN, VA) | $1,100 | $1,600 | $2,400 |
| South/Gulf (TX, LA, FL, AL, MS) | $1,800 | $3,000 | $5,000+ |
| Southwest (AZ, NM, NV) | $800 | $1,200 | $1,700 |
| West (CA, WA, OR) | $1,200 | $2,000 | $3,500 |
| Mountain (CO, UT, ID, MT) | $900 | $1,400 | $2,000 |

Note: FL, TX Gulf Coast, and CA fire zones can be 2–5x higher.

---

## Multifamily Insurance (Annual, Per Unit)

| Region | Per-Unit Annual |
|---|---|
| Midwest | $400–$700 |
| Northeast | $600–$1,100 |
| Southeast | $500–$900 |
| South/Gulf Coast | $900–$2,000+ |
| Southwest | $400–$700 |
| West | $600–$1,200 |

Multiply per-unit estimate by number of units for total building insurance.
Add ~$200–$500 for umbrella/liability coverage.

---

## Default to Use When Region is Unknown

SFR: $1,500/yr
Per unit (MF): $700/unit/yr

Always note: "Insurance estimated — recommend landlord insurance quote before closing."

---

## Flood Insurance

If property is in a FEMA flood zone (Zone A, AE, VE):
- Add $800–$3,000/yr for NFIP or private flood policy
- Flag this as a significant risk item in the report
- Search: `"[address] FEMA flood zone"` to check
