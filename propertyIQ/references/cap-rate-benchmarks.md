# Cap Rate Benchmarks by Market Tier

Cap rates vary significantly by market. Use these as context when evaluating a deal.
Always search for current local market data to supplement these benchmarks.

```
web_search: "[city] [state] multifamily cap rate [year]"
web_search: "[city] SFR cap rate market [year]"
```

---

## Multifamily Cap Rates (2024–2025 Estimates)

### Tier 1 — Major Gateway Markets
(NYC, LA, SF, Boston, Seattle, DC, Miami)
- Cap Rate Range: 3.5–5.5%
- Deals above 5.5% are unusual — verify rents/expenses
- These are appreciation markets; cash flow is thin by design

### Tier 2 — Large Secondary Markets
(Chicago, Dallas, Houston, Atlanta, Phoenix, Denver, Charlotte, Nashville, Austin)
- Cap Rate Range: 5.0–6.5%
- 6%+ is solid; 5% is market-rate
- Cash flow positive deals are achievable with right financing

### Tier 3 — Mid-Size Markets
(Columbus, Indianapolis, Kansas City, Memphis, Cleveland, Baltimore, Pittsburgh, NJ suburbs)
- Cap Rate Range: 6.0–8.5%
- NJ specifically: 6.5–8.5% for multifamily
- Strong cash flow markets; higher risk from vacancy/mgmt

### Tier 4 — Small/Rural Markets
(Sub-100k population cities, rural areas)
- Cap Rate Range: 8–12%+
- High yield but limited liquidity and appreciation
- Harder to exit; factor in holding period carefully

---

## SFR Cap Rates

SFR cap rates run ~0.5–1.0% lower than comparable multifamily due to simpler management
and owner-occupant exit potential.

- Tier 1: 3.0–4.5%
- Tier 2: 4.5–6.0%
- Tier 3: 5.5–7.5%
- Tier 4: 7.0–10%+

---

## Deal Quality Signals

| Cap Rate vs. Market | Signal |
|---|---|
| >1.5% above market | Investigate — rents may be above market, expenses understated, or deferred maintenance |
| 0.5–1.5% above market | Good deal or value-add opportunity |
| At market rate | Fair price; returns depend on financing and rent growth |
| Below market | Paying for appreciation/location; cash flow will be thin |

---

## GRM (Gross Rent Multiplier) Benchmarks

GRM = Purchase Price / Annual Gross Rent

Lower GRM = better value for investor

| Market Tier | Typical GRM Range |
|---|---|
| Tier 1 | 18–28x |
| Tier 2 | 12–18x |
| Tier 3 | 8–14x |
| Tier 4 | 5–10x |

A GRM above 15x in a Tier 3 market warrants scrutiny.

## DSCR Thresholds

| DSCR | Lender/Investor Interpretation |
|---|---|
| < 1.00 | NOI doesn't cover debt — not lendable |
| 1.00–1.20 | Technically serviceable but tight — most lenders require 1.20x minimum |
| 1.20–1.35 | Acceptable — meets most commercial lender minimums |
| 1.35–1.50 | Good — solid cushion |
| > 1.50 | Strong — comfortable cash flow |
